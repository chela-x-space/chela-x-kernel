# K6.5 Workflow Step Coordination

## Status
Draft

## Purpose
Define how K6 coordinates bounded workflow stages without performing business work.

## Scope
- Ordered workflow stages, entry and exit criteria, dependencies, waiting conditions, and produced evidence.
- Coordination requests that may later trigger task creation, event waiting, or approved downstream outcomes.

## Out of Scope
- Task ownership and assignment logic.
- Business execution logic.
- Scheduler loops, queue consumers, timers, or infrastructure runners.

## Canonical Terminology
- Workflow Stage: one named coordination segment with explicit prerequisites and outputs.
- Entry Criteria: the prerequisites required before a stage may become active.
- Exit Criteria: the conditions required before the next stage may be requested.

## Structures
- Ordered stage-set declarations bound to one approved workflow definition.
- Stage transition graph declarations that remain bounded and replayable.
- Waiting-state transitions using the existing `WorkflowState::Waiting` lifecycle state.

## Invariants
- Every workflow definition declares one ordered stage set.
- Stage logic consumes upstream outcomes; it does not create new authority.
- Stage graphs are acyclic except for explicitly bounded retry or recovery paths.
- Waiting does not imply execution; it preserves a governed pause for external approved input.

## Validation Precedence
1. Stage identity and ordering completeness.
2. Entry and exit criteria completeness.
3. Participant and upstream-evidence prerequisites.
4. Transition-graph boundedness and retry-cycle checks.
5. Waiting, resume, completion, and cancellation compatibility with the workflow state machine.

## Deterministic Outcomes
- Stage activation and coordination decisions depend only on declared criteria and validated upstream outcomes.
- Equivalent stage graphs yield equivalent validation and replay results.
- No implicit background execution is introduced by stage coordination.

## K1-K5 Relationships
- K2 supplies `Waiting`, `Running`, `Paused`, `Completed`, and related lifecycle controls.
- K3 supplies participant, delegation, SoD, and policy constraints consumed by stages.
- K5 supplies workflow events and replay rules used to preserve stage-history meaning.
- K7 will later own tasks that K6 may request but does not define here.

## CES Traceability
- `CES-B0-030.8`: ordered stage sets, entry and exit criteria, bounded transitions.
- `CES-B0-030.9`: waiting-state and transition compatibility.
- `CES-B0-030.14`: bounded retry and recovery loops only.
- `CES-B0-030.18`: no unbounded circular execution.

## API Implications
- Future K6 coordination APIs should remain declarative and immutable.
- Coordination must be representable without introducing schedulers, async traits, or queue contracts.
- Waiting-for-event semantics should consume K5 facts rather than infrastructure notifications.

## Acceptance Criteria
- Workflow step coordination remains purely orchestration and never performs business work.
- Stage graphs are explicit, bounded, and compatible with replay.
- The K6 step model leaves task and execution semantics to K7 and K8.

## Final Determination
K6 step coordination is the bounded stage-orchestration model that requests downstream work and waits on approved facts without becoming a task engine or execution engine.
